Question 1 of 46
Look at the photo carefully before choosing your answer.

Look at the photo and choose the statement that best describes what you see.

AThe man is sitting at a desk and reading documents.
BA man is speaking with a woman at a reception counter.
CTwo women are arranging plants near the entrance.
DThe lobby is empty and the lights are off.
Correct: B — The photo shows a man talking with a woman at the reception desk. A is wrong (he is standing, not sitting). C is wrong (the women aren't arranging plants). D is wrong (the lobby is not empty).
Question 2 of 46
Look at the photo carefully before choosing your answer.

Choose the statement that best describes the photo.

AGuests are enjoying a meal at a restaurant.
BA waiter is taking an order from a customer.
CA table is being set with glasses and cutlery.
DFood is being served on a tray.
Correct: C — The table is being prepared (set) before guests arrive. A and B require guests, which are absent. D describes serving food, not setting a table.
Question 3 of 46
Look at the photo carefully before choosing your answer.

AWorkers are loading boxes onto a delivery truck outside.
BThe warehouse shelves are completely empty.
CWorkers in safety vests are moving goods with forklifts inside a warehouse.
DA single worker is painting the walls of the storage room.
Correct: C — The photo depicts workers using forklifts in a warehouse with racks. A (outside truck) and D (painting) are not shown. B is wrong because shelves contain goods.

Reading — Part 5

Incomplete Sentences (10 ข้อ)

เลือกคำหรือวลีที่เหมาะสมที่สุดเพื่อเติมประโยคให้สมบูรณ์

Question 21 of 46

The marketing team _______ the new campaign strategy at yesterday's meeting.

Apresents
Bpresented
Cwill present
Dpresenting
Correct: B — "yesterday's meeting" = past time marker → simple past tense "presented" is required.
Question 22 of 46

All employees are required to submit their expense reports _______ the 15th of each month.

Auntil
Bduring
Cby
Dsince
Correct: C — "by" = no later than a deadline. "Until" implies continuous action. "During" and "since" don't fit deadline usage.
Question 23 of 46

The proposal _______ by the board before the end of the quarter.

Awill approve
Bhas approved
Cmust be approved
Dapproves
Correct: C — "The proposal" is the object of approval → passive voice needed. "must be approved" = passive modal, fitting the deadline context.
Question 24 of 46

Mr. Tanaka is responsible _______ overseeing the quality assurance process.

Ato
Bfor
Cin
Dat
Correct: B — Fixed collocation: "responsible FOR" doing something. This is a standard preposition that must be memorized.
Question 25 of 46

The software update _______ significant improvements to the user interface.

Aincluding
Binclude
Cincludes
Dto include
Correct: C — "The software update" = third-person singular subject → verb needs "s" → "includes".
Question 26 of 46

_______ the meeting ran overtime, all agenda items were covered.

ABecause
BAlthough
CSince
DUnless
Correct: B — "Although" introduces a contrast (ran overtime BUT items were covered). "Because/Since" would cause a logical contradiction. "Unless" implies a condition.
Question 27 of 46

She has worked in the testing field _______ over ten years.

Asince
Bduring
Cfor
Dat
Correct: C — "For" is used with a duration/length of time ("ten years"). "Since" is used with a point in time ("since 2010").
Question 28 of 46

The _______ report showed a 15% reduction in bug rates after the new process was implemented.

Amonth
Bmonthly
Cmonths
Dmonthful
Correct: B — An adjective is needed before "report" → "monthly" (adjective). "Month/months" are nouns, "monthful" is not a real word.
Question 29 of 46

Customers are encouraged to contact customer service _______ they experience any issues.

Awhenever
Bwherever
Cwhatever
Dhowever
Correct: A — "Whenever" = at any time that (temporal conjunction). "Wherever" = place, "whatever" = thing, "however" = manner — none fit a time-condition context.
Question 30 of 46

The _______ of the new testing framework exceeded everyone's expectations.

Aeffective
Beffectively
Ceffectiveness
Deffecting
Correct: C — The blank is the subject of the sentence → needs a noun. "effectiveness" (noun). "effective" = adjective, "effectively" = adverb.
Reading — Part 6

Text Completion (4 ข้อ)

อ่านข้อความแล้วเลือกคำหรือวลีที่เหมาะสมที่สุดเพื่อเติมช่องว่าง

Memo — To: All Staff / From: HR Department / Re: Annual Performance Reviews

This is a reminder that annual performance reviews will begin on November 1st. All managers are (Q25) _______ to schedule one-on-one meetings with their team members by October 25th. Please ensure that self-evaluation forms have been completed and (Q26) _______ to your direct supervisor before the review date. Employees who (Q27) _______ to submit their forms on time may face delays in processing their reviews. (Q28) _______, the HR department will be available to answer questions every Thursday from 2–4 PM.
Question 31 of 46

Select the best word for blank (Q25).

Aasked
Bask
Casking
Dto ask
Correct: A — "are asked" = passive voice (be + past participle). "are asking" would also work grammatically but means something different. "are asked to" = requested to.
Question 32 of 46

Select the best word for blank (Q26).

Asubmit
Bsubmitted
Csubmitting
Dsubmission
Correct: B — "have been completed and submitted" = parallel passive structure (both past participles after "have been").
Question 33 of 46

Select the best word for blank (Q27).

Afail
Bfailed
Cfailing
Dto fail
Correct: A — "Employees who fail to submit" = present tense relative clause describing a general condition/policy.
Question 34 of 46

Select the best word for blank (Q28).

AHowever
BTherefore
CIn addition
DAs a result
Correct: C — "In addition" adds supplementary information (HR availability). The previous sentences give instructions; this adds another piece of helpful info.

Email — Questions 35–38
Email
From: jessica.moore@techsolutions.com
To: allstaff@techsolutions.com
Subject: Office Renovation — Temporary Workspace Changes

Dear colleagues,

I am writing to inform you of upcoming changes to our workspace. Starting Monday, October 14th, the third and fourth floors will be closed for renovation. This project is expected to last approximately three weeks.

During this period, affected staff will be relocated to the co-working spaces on the first floor. Please note that desk assignments will be communicated by your direct managers by end of day Friday. Employees who need to access files stored on the third or fourth floor must request assistance from the facilities team at least 48 hours in advance.

We apologize for any inconvenience and appreciate your cooperation. A catered lunch will be provided on October 14th to thank you for your flexibility.

Best regards,
Jessica Moore, Office Operations Manager

Article — Questions 39–42
The Rise of AI in Software Testing

Artificial intelligence is rapidly transforming the field of software quality assurance. Traditional manual testing, while still valuable, is increasingly being supplemented by AI-powered tools that can identify bugs, predict failure points, and generate test cases automatically.

According to a 2024 industry report, companies that adopted AI-assisted testing reduced their defect escape rate by an average of 32%. Furthermore, test execution time dropped by nearly 45% in organizations using machine learning-based regression test selection.

However, experts caution that AI tools are not a replacement for skilled QA engineers. Human testers bring creativity, contextual judgment, and an understanding of user experience that current AI systems cannot fully replicate. The consensus in the industry is that the future of QA lies in a hybrid approach — combining AI efficiency with human insight.
